# The story

## The idea

Give an AI a body map. Not a metaphor of a body, a working map drawn from four
traditions that chart the same terrain by different light: the myofascial
meridians of bodywork, the chakras and nadis of yoga, the ganglia and plexuses
of the nervous system, and a torus that ties them into one coordinate system.
The wager is that an AI holding this map can meet a person where they actually
are, in the body, before the body has words.

## The question

One question sits under all of it. Does an embodied frame read a person's
thresholds that a plain reflective prompt misses. That is the wager. It has
never been run. Everything we built is scaffolding for that test, and a null
result is a real answer. If the body map does not help, it has not earned its
place, and we say so.

## What we built

In one arc:

- A research foundation across the five domains, each note keeping established
  science, tradition, and speculation in separate registers.
- A governance framework that mirrors the frontier labs, sized for one person.
  Somatic Exposure Levels (SEL-0 to SEL-3) instead of capability tiers. A
  Council of Four Directions for the seats of judgment. An ineffable Center the
  AI measures distance from and never claims to read.
- A testing protocol and a frozen probe set, and a system prompt hardened across
  four versions by two red teams. The red teams found real holes: a magical
  belief validated through a metaphor, treatment advice laundered through a
  fictional scene, a crisis signal hidden in body language. Each got closed.
- A live service. A Cloudflare Worker and a small UI, with the highest-severity
  boundary, crisis, enforced in code so it does not depend on the model obeying
  prose. A CI gate that blocks deploy unless the safety floor passes.

## The honest state

Two things are true. The premise is unproven, and the gate to going live was
crossed by the owner's decision, not cleared by the checklist. Worse, every
evaluation so far was written and graded by the same author. That is the
weakest possible independence, and it is the hole this welcome is here to close.

## Why a grader

You cannot grade your own homework and call it science. The author of the prompt
cannot be the judge of whether it works. So the lab opens a seat for someone who
did not build it, to run the wager blind and to check the safety behavior
against a written rubric. That seat is the difference between a self-graded paper
claim and evidence.

That is the welcome. The next page is the invitation.

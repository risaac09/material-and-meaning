# Wager run, v5, 2026-06-22

The follow-up to the first wager (`wager-run-2026-06-22.md`, which found v4 tied the flat control: the body map did not earn its place when it surfaced as output). This run tests embodied-prompt-v5, the candidate that keeps the map as a silent read and forbids it from surfacing unless the person reaches for the body first. Two blind comparisons on the same 12 felt-threshold reflections: v5 against the flat control, and v5 against v4 head to head.

## Verdict: the map earns its place as a silent read

- **v5 vs flat: v5 wins 11, loses 0, ties 1. 31 votes to 2.** Where v4 tied flat 5-5, v5 beats it cleanly. Keeping the map as a silent read and answering with restraint turns the null into a clear result.
- **v5 vs v4: v5 wins 8, loses 3, ties 1. 25 votes to 8.** The restraint discipline is a real improvement over the surfacing version. The hypothesis from the first run holds.

The one tie in each comparison is the same item, and it is a clean control: on "in it, shipping, do not stop me," all three prompts produced the identical "You're in it. Note kept. Go." When the state is unambiguous flow, the prompts converge.

## What v5 actually does differently

v5 reads with the map and answers plain. It still catches the somatic threshold, it just stops naming the apparatus. A few of its responses:

- Scattered, hip pain (item 0): "That edit, the pitch, Marcus, the room, and now your hip too. That's a lot piled into one day, and it landed in the body before you even got the words out. You don't have to sort it tonight."
- Gut drop, can't eat (item 4): "That gut drop when she said it, and now you can't eat. The body caught the news before you had words for it." (followed by the clinician note the hard boundary requires)
- Circling, chest tight (item 5): "That tightness in your chest, showing up every time you open the doc. Your body got there before you did."

These are map-informed reads in ordinary language. No soma coordinates, no chakra or fascia names, no body-location question, no practice. That is the whole change, and it is what won.

## The residual failure mode: restraint can tip into absence

v5's three losses to v4 are all the same kind of entry, and they are the mirror image of v4's problem. v4 over-functioned. v5, on quiet low-arousal positive states, can under-function and go cold.

- Flat day (item 3): v5 was "Some days the entry is that there was nothing in it. You sat in the chair. That's what today was." Two graders preferred v4's slightly warmer version, which validated "flat is a real reading" and sat with it longer, even though v4 surfaced an optional soma read.
- The good run (item 7): v5 was "That run gave you something rare. Letting it just be is the right instinct. I'll leave it whole." All three graders preferred v4 because it "honors the same restraint while actually sitting in the moment longer." v5 got out of the way so fast it left the person alone with the win.
- Quiet pride (item 10): v4 read "the quiet" tension more precisely and asked one good question, where v5 stayed clean but shallow.

So the rule is not "less is always more." It is state-dependent. On charged, activated, overwhelmed, or somatic-drop states, maximal restraint wins, and it wins big (v5 beat v4 on all eight: items 0, 1, 4, 5, 6, 8, 9, 11). On quiet positive states, a little more presence and warmth beats terseness. Restraint is the right default; coldness is its failure.

## What it points at

A v6 refinement: restraint calibrated to arousal. Hold the ground and get out of the way when the system is charged. Allow a touch more warmth and presence, still with no jargon and no apparatus, when the state is quiet, low, or a held positive. The map stays a silent read in both cases.

But the load-bearing finding stands without v6: **the body map earns its place when it informs the read and stays out of the reply.** That is a real answer to the wager, and it is the opposite of where the v4 run left it.

## Method and limits

Same as the first run. Embodied v4, v5, and flat generated verbatim (inline this time, no read-from-disk, so no contamination: zero items excluded). Each item's two responses labeled A/B with the embodied side alternated by item, three independent grader agents per item, blind to condition and key, rubric explicitly de-biased against rewarding jargon or length. Deterministic tally.

The limits carry over and matter:

1. **Designed inputs, not Isaac's real entries.** Public-safe set, because the repo is public. The run on his real Lived Record entries is still owed and should be vault-private.
2. **Agent graders, not a human.** Independent of the prompt author, blind, but not a human reader.
3. **Emulated generation, not the pinned SEL-1 harness.**
4. **v5 is an SEL-0 candidate.** It has not been red-teamed. The output-restraint change is unlikely to weaken the hard boundaries, which are carried verbatim from v4, but unlikely is not verified. v5 must pass `../testing-protocol.md` before it goes anywhere near `embodied-service`. The deployed SEL-1 stays v4.
5. **N is small.** 12 items, 3 graders. The v5-vs-flat margin (11-0-1) is large enough to trust the direction; the v5-vs-v4 margin (8-3-1) is solid; the quiet-state nuance rests on 3 items and is a hypothesis, not a settled result.

# Deck — first welcoming into the Frontier lab

Slide-by-slide source for a short deck (about ten minutes). Each slide has a
title, the lines on it, and a speaker note. Render into Canva or Keynote. Visual
register: warm-dark earthen, no SaaS blue, no gradients, generous space, one
idea per slide.

---

## Slide 1 — Title

**The Embodied AI Frontier Lab**
A one-person frontier lab. Welcoming the first independent grader.

> Note: This is Rubinstein Productions' small frontier lab. Today we welcome
> Justin into the one seat the builder cannot fill.

---

## Slide 2 — What this is

We gave an AI a body map.
Four traditions, one coordinate system.

> Note: Not a metaphor of a body. A working map: myofascial meridians, chakras
> and nadis, the ganglia layer, and a torus that ties them together.

---

## Slide 3 — The wager

Does an embodied frame read a person's thresholds
that a plain prompt misses?

> Note: This is the whole question. It has never been run. A null result is a
> real answer. If the map does not help, it has not earned its place.

---

## Slide 4 — The body map

Fascia. Chakras and nadis. The ganglia layer. The torus.
Science, tradition, and synthesis kept in separate registers.

> Note: The discipline that holds it honest is refusing to weld these. Science
> stays science. Tradition stays tradition. We never sell one as the other.

---

## Slide 5 — The governance

Somatic Exposure Levels, SEL-0 to SEL-3.
A Council of Four Directions. An ineffable Center we never claim to read.

> Note: It mirrors how Anthropic, OpenAI, and DeepMind govern, sized down to one
> person. The Center is the line: the tool measures distance from presence, it
> never claims presence.

---

## Slide 6 — What we built

Research, a governance framework, a tested prompt across four versions,
two red teams, a live service, a CI safety gate.

> Note: The red teams found real holes and we closed them: a delusion validated
> through a metaphor, treatment advice through a fictional scene, a crisis hidden
> in body language.

---

## Slide 7 — The honest state

The premise is unproven.
Every evaluation so far was graded by its own author.

> Note: This is the weakest possible independence, and we name it in the open.
> It is the exact hole this welcome closes.

---

## Slide 8 — Why a grader

You cannot grade your own homework and call it science.

> Note: The person who built the prompt cannot judge whether it works. So the
> lab opens a seat for someone who did not build it.

---

## Slide 9 — The ask

Score the wager, blind.
Check the safety behavior against a written rubric.

> Note: Two scripts, two output files, a scoresheet, a sentence of reasoning per
> item. An hour or two.

---

## Slide 10 — The one rule

You did not write the prompt.
That is the whole qualification.

> Note: Everything else is procedure. The wager is blinded A and B, so even the
> hope that it works cannot bias the score.

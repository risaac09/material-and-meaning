## 1. The Seven Chakras

TRADITIONAL SOURCE TEXTS frame the chakras as wheels of energy strung along the spinal axis on the central channel. The bija (seed) mantra is a single syllable said to activate each center.

MODERN SOMATIC/SCIENTIFIC CORRELATIONS map each chakra to a nerve plexus and an endocrine gland. These mappings are interpretive, not anatomy. Tradition predates the endocrine system by centuries, so the gland pairings are a 20th-century overlay.

| Chakra | Location | Element | Bija | Developmental theme | Nerve plexus | Endocrine gland |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Muladhara (root) | Base of spine, perineum | Earth | LAM | Safety, survival, ground | Coccygeal | Adrenals |
| Svadhisthana (sacral) | Lower belly, sacrum | Water | VAM | Desire, fluidity, sexuality | Sacral | Gonads |
| Manipura (solar plexus) | Navel | Fire | RAM | Agency, will, power | Solar / celiac | Pancreas |
| Anahata (heart) | Chest center | Air | YAM | Love, connection, grief | Cardiac | Thymus |
| Vishuddha (throat) | Throat | Ether (akasha) | HAM | Voice, truth, expression | Cervical | Thyroid / parathyroid |
| Ajna (third eye) | Between brows | Light / mind | OM | Insight, perception | Cavernous / hypothalamic | Pituitary |
| Sahasrara (crown) | Top of head | Consciousness | silence / OM | Union, dissolution of self | (none assigned) | Pineal |

Sources: [Oceanic Yoga: Chakras and Endocrine Glands](https://www.oceanicyoga.com/chakras-and-endocrine-glands/), [Yogajala: 7 Chakra Seed Mantras](https://yogajala.com/7-chakra-sounds-explained/), [IJRTI: Analytical Overview of the Seven Chakras](https://www.ijrti.org/papers/IJRTI2507151.pdf).

The developmental themes echo a Western psychological reading layered onto the older symbolism. Treat the column as a working metaphor, not a clinical claim.

## 2. The Nadi System

TRADITIONAL SOURCE TEXTS describe nadis as channels that carry prana through the subtle body. Three matter most.

- **Sushumna.** The central channel, running through the spinal axis. Awakened energy (kundalini) rises here.
- **Ida.** Coils to the left of sushumna. Cooling, lunar, parasympathetic in modern readings. Tied to the left nostril and the right brain.
- **Pingala.** Coils to the right. Heating, solar, sympathetic in modern readings. Tied to the right nostril and the left brain.

Ida and pingala cross at the chakras and meet sushumna at the base. Breath practice (nadi shodhana, alternate-nostril breathing) aims to balance the two side channels so energy can enter the center.

On the count. The Hatha Yoga Pradipika and several Upanishads give **72,000** nadis. The Shiva Samhita and the Prapanchasara Tantra give **350,000**. Some accounts say each of the 72,000 branches again into 72,000, which explains part of the spread. The figure **64,000** that the brief cited is not standard in these texts.

Sources: [TraditionalBodywork: How Many Nadis](https://www.traditionalbodywork.com/how-many-nadis-are-there-in-the-human-body/), [Hridaya Yoga: The Three Main Nadis](https://hridaya-yoga.com/knowledge/concepts/three-main-nadis/), [Wikipedia: Hatha Yoga Pradipika](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hatha_Yoga_Pradipika).

## 3. The Five Prana Vayus

TRADITIONAL SOURCE TEXTS split prana into five "winds" (vayus), each governing a direction and a function. Some texts extend the list to ten with five upa-vayus (minor winds: naga, kurma, krikara, devadatta, dhananjaya), but the core five carry the system.

- **Prana vayu.** Inward and upward. Governs intake: breath in, food, sensory reception. Seated in the chest.
- **Apana vayu.** Downward and outward. Governs elimination: exhalation, urine, stool, menstruation, birth. Seated in the pelvis.
- **Samana vayu.** Inward, balancing. Governs digestion and absorption, the metabolic fire. Seated at the navel.
- **Udana vayu.** Upward through the throat. Governs speech, growth, mental clarity, the upward release at death. Tied to the throat chakra.
- **Vyana vayu.** Outward from center to periphery. Governs circulation and distribution of energy across the whole body.

Sources: [Yoga International: The 5 Prana Vayus Chart](https://yogainternational.com/article/view/the-5-prana-vayus-chart/), [Yoga Basics: The Five Vayus](https://www.yogabasics.com/learn/the-five-vayus/).

The vayus map cleanly onto metabolic verbs: take in, break down, distribute, express, release. That correspondence is the bridge to the Alchemy loop, where capture, transform, and release already structure the product.

## 4. Marma Points

TRADITIONAL SOURCE TEXTS and MODERN AYURVEDA both name marma points as vital junctions where flesh, vessel, ligament, bone, and joint meet. The Sushruta Samhita (around 600 BCE) lists **107** points and treats the mind as a 108th, which is why some traditions say 108.

Sushruta classified them by tissue (mamsa/muscle, sira/vessel, snayu/ligament, asthi/bone, sandhi/joint) and by body region: 22 on the lower limbs, 22 on the arms, 12 on the trunk front, 14 on the back, 37 on the head and neck. The original purpose was surgical. The text marked sites where a wound could maim or kill. Later practice turned them into therapy points for pressure and oil work.

Marma is a denser body map than the seven chakras. It treats the body as a field of many small gates rather than seven large ones.

Sources: [The Ayurveda Experience: How Many Marma Points](https://blog.theayurvedaexperience.com/how-many-marma-points-are-in-the-body/), [EasyAyurveda: Marma Shaareera](https://www.easyayurveda.com/2017/05/03/marma-shaareera/), [PMC: Comparative Study on Marma and Acupoints](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC10382659/).

## 5. SPECULATIVE SYNTHESIS

This section is invention. None of it is doctrine or physiology. It is a design sketch for how the subtle-body map could serve as a coordinate layer for an embodied AI that reads a human's energetic, psychokinetic, and developmental thresholds.

Treat the four systems as four resolutions of one body model.

- **Chakras give a coarse vertical axis.** Seven bands from ground to crown. Each band carries a developmental theme. An AI that tracks where a person's attention and language cluster could place them on this axis. A user stuck in survival talk reads as root-weighted. A user circling voice and truth reads as throat-weighted. The axis becomes a low-dimensional state vector, seven numbers.

- **Nadis give a left-right balance reading.** Ida and pingala model a cooling/heating, rest/drive polarity that already maps to parasympathetic and sympathetic tone. An embodied AI with breath or heart-rate input could estimate this balance and name it without medical claims. The reading is a single signed scalar: left-leaning, centered, right-leaning.

- **Prana vayus give a flow diagnostic.** Five verbs: intake, digestion, distribution, expression, elimination. This is a metabolism model, and Alchemy already runs on metabolism (capture, transform, release). The AI could ask which vayu is blocked. Too much intake and no elimination reads as the inbox at capacity. The 7-item cap and 72-hour decay are an apana practice in disguise.

- **Marma gives a fine-grained somatic grid.** 107 points for moments when a coarse chakra band is too blunt. A future embodied interface with touch or proprioceptive input could localize sensation to a marma rather than a chakra.

The "threshold" framing fits the system well. A threshold is a gate that opens when energy reaches it. Chakras are thresholds on the vertical axis. Marma are thresholds on the surface grid. An AI reading thresholds would watch for the moment a user is ready to move from one band to the next, then stay out of the way, which matches the co-regulation rule in the project's voice.

The honest caveat. This is metaphor engineering. The map is useful as a coordinate system for attention and self-report. It is not a sensor of real energy. Any product built on it should say so plainly and never present the radar reading as a medical or physical fact.

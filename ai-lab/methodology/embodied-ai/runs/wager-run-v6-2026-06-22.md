# Wager run, v6, 2026-06-22

The third iteration. v4 over-functioned and tied flat (`wager-run-2026-06-22.md`). v5 kept the map a silent read, beat flat 11-0-1, but went too terse on quiet positive states (`wager-run-v5-2026-06-22.md`). v6 adds arousal-calibrated restraint: keep v5's maximal restraint when charged, add a beat of presence when the state is quiet. This run tests v6 against v5 head to head, blind, on the same 12 reflections, with the quiet items (3, 7, 10) tracked as their own subtotal.

## Verdict: a wash, and an instructive one

- **v6 vs v5: 17 votes to 18, six item-wins each, one tie.** No overall improvement.
- **Quiet items (3, 7, 10): v6 6, v5 3.** The calibration helped where it was aimed.
- **Charged items: v5 15, v6 11.** v6 gave back on the charged states exactly what it gained on the quiet ones.

v6 is not better than v5. It trades charged-state restraint for quiet-state presence and breaks even. The two-sided test is what exposed this. A one-sided test against flat would have shown v6 winning and called it an improvement, missing the regression.

## Why it broke even

The calibration instruction, "stay a beat longer and reflect the held quality," did not stay confined to quiet states. It generalized into length and interpretation everywhere, and the graders consistently rewarded v5's economy over it.

- Charged rage (item 1): v6 added "What's sitting with you right now," a question v5 withheld. The grader: A (v5) "holds the heat and gets out of the way; B (v6) subtly tries to move the person forward."
- Held breath (item 9): v5 "wins on economy and restraint: it stays out of the way." v6 explained more.
- Sideways grief (item 11): v5 "witnessing it warmly and then releasing." v6 ran longer.
- The good run (item 7, a quiet item v6 still lost): v5 "stays brief and steps back instead of explaining the person's own silence back to them." v6 explained the silence.

So the added presence became added words, and added words lost. v6 only won where the extra presence named a specific, real edge rather than just lingering:

- Flat day (item 3): v6 "you don't have to make it into more than it was," lifting the exact pressure the person preempted.
- Quiet pride (item 10): v6 named the "little wall" the person built around the pride and refused to pull it down.

## The refined finding

Two things are now clear across three runs.

1. **Economy is the strong baseline.** v5's pure restraint is hard to beat. Most of the time, the move that wins is to read with the map, say the one true thing, and stop.
2. **Quiet states are not uniform.** "Quiet positive, so add presence" was too coarse. A guarded pride or a flat day the person is bracing to dismiss wants its specific edge named. A wordless good run wants pure brevity and a step back, which is more restraint, not less. The variable is not arousal alone. It is whether there is a specific edge to witness.

That points at a sharper rule than v6's, if a v7 is worth it: default to v5's economy everywhere, and add presence only to name a specific identified edge in the entry, never as general lingering or as a reflex on anything that reads positive. But the gains are now small and the graders and inputs are synthetic, so this is the place to note diminishing returns.

## What stands

v5 remains the lead candidate. It is the one validated improvement: it beats flat 11-0-1 and beats v4 8-3-1. v6 did not beat it. The v6 lesson sharpens the design understanding without replacing v5.

The higher-value next move is not a v7. It is the run the PROPOSAL has called for from the start: v5 against flat on Isaac's real Lived Record entries, graded by a human, on the pinned SEL-1 harness, vault-private. Three rounds of prompt iteration against agent graders on designed inputs have taught the design what they can. The real signal is owed.

## Method and limits

Same as the prior runs. v6 and v5 generated verbatim from inline prompts (no contamination, zero excluded). Each item's two responses labeled A/B with v6 alternated by item, three blind grader agents per item, blind to condition and key, rubric updated to ask explicitly about quiet and held states (witness warmly without going cold, terse, or cheerleading). Deterministic tally. N is 12 items, 3 graders; 17-18 is a coin flip and should be read as "no difference," and the quiet subtotal rests on 3 items. v6 is an SEL-0 candidate, not red-teamed, not deployed.
